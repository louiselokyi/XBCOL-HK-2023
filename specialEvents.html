IBCOL Special Events

BCHX / Exam


BCHX / exam is an essential step for those who have completed IBCOL workshops and training. This exam is designed to test the proficiency and knowledge in implementing blockchain technology to solve nowadays industry problems. The exam covers a broad range of topics, including IBCOL beginner and advance course topics. Passing this exam demonstrates a high level of expertise in the field of blockchain in healthcare, making candidates valuable assets to their organizations.

GBA IBCOL: (Sep 16, 2023)


The International Blockchain Olympiad (IBCOL) is an annual global competition, inviting students from all over the world, including Hong Kong, China and Macao to propose solutions for real-world challenges with blockchain technology.

The theme of IBCOL is the 17 sustainable development goals (SDGs) set forth by the United Nations. Participants with promising concepts, have a shot at commercialising their project with the funding that’s up for grabs for the winners of this competition.

Amsterdam 2023: (Nov 16 - 18, 2023)


Nominated teams from around the world will have the opportunity to showcase their ideas and projects in the finals in Amsterdam. The event will be in the form of an exhibition, and each team will have a designated area to display their poster board and videos. The posters will showcase the team's project, including the technical details, advantages, and potential impact of their work. There will be the opportunity to interact with attendees, who will include industry experts, academics, judges and other teams.

The Final Rounds of the International Blockchain Olympiad (IBCOL Finals) is held in the vibrant city of Amsterdam this year. Amsterdam's leading position in innovation and technology makes it the perfect location for IBCOL Finals. Not only is the city home to a thriving blockchain startup ecosystem, but the Dutch government has also established a favourable regulatory environment for blockchain technology. This relationship between Amsterdam and the blockchain industry creates an ideal environment for the IBCOL Finals.

  • How to get to Amsterdam

    1. Travel Requirements
      Visa applications may be required. It's important to note that visa requirements can change, so it's always a good idea to check with the Netherlands embassy or consulate in your home country before you travel to Amsterdam.
      Amsterdam is located in the Netherlands. The Netherlands is part of the Schengen Area, which is a zone of 26 European countries that have abolished passport and other types of border control at their mutual borders.
      If you are a citizen of a country that is not a member of the European Union (EU) or European Economic Area (EEA), you may need to obtain a visa before you can enter the Schengen Area, which includes Amsterdam. Some examples of countries whose citizens need a visa to enter the Netherlands include:
      - Afghanistan
      - Bangladesh
      - China
      - India
      - Iraq
      - Nigeria
      - Pakistan
      - Philippines
      - Russia
      - Saudi Arabia
      - Turkey
      - Ukraine

      Additionally, even if you do not need a visa to enter the Netherlands, you may need to meet other requirements, such as having a valid passport or travel insurance.
      Note there are no travel restrictions related to COVID in Amsterdam. The Government of the Netherlands has decided to lift all travel restrictions related to the Coronavirus: https://www.government.nl/topics/coronavirus-covid-19/visiting-the-netherlands-from-abroad


    2. Transportation
      By Plane: Amsterdam has a major international airport, Schiphol Airport, which is located about 20 minutes by train from Amsterdam city centre. Schiphol Airport is well-connected to many cities around the world, and there are numerous airlines that offer direct flights to Amsterdam.
      (a) By Plane: Amsterdam has a major international airport, Schiphol Airport, which is located about 20 minutes by train from Amsterdam city centre. Schiphol Airport is well-connected to many cities around the world, and there are numerous airlines that offer direct flights to Amsterdam.
      (b) By Train: Amsterdam is well-connected by train to many European cities, including Paris, Berlin, and Brussels. The main train station in Amsterdam is Amsterdam Centraal, which is located in the city centre and is easily accessible by public transportation.
      (c) By Car: If you prefer to drive, Amsterdam is easily accessible by car from many nearby cities. However, keep in mind that parking in Amsterdam can be expensive, and the city centre is pedestrian-friendly, so driving may not be the best option for getting around the city.
      (d) By Bus: There are several bus companies that offer affordable transportation to Amsterdam from nearby cities. However, the travel time can be longer than other methods of transportation.
      (e) By Boat: Amsterdam is also accessible by boat, with several cruise ships and ferries arriving at the city's port. The port is located just outside the city centre, but is easily accessible by public transportation.
