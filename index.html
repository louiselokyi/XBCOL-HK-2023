About

The Hong Kong Blockchain Olympiad (HKBCOL) is an annual competition inviting current and recent students to propose solutions to real-world problems using blockchain technology. The Olympiad combines the practicality of a traditional case competitions with the open-ended problem-solving culture of hack-a-thons. As a team of up to 5 students, competitors submit their case using an academic conference style white-paper along with a pitch and presentation deck.

This year, the Hong Kong Blockchain Olympiad will merge with the Macau Blockchain Olympiad and China Blockchain Olympiad, to host the first every Blockchain Olympiad in the Greater Bay Area. Instead of competing in their own regional Olympiad, competitors from all three regions will submit and compete in one big cross regional Olympiad.

High performing teams from this IBCOL in GBA finals will then be invited to compete in the International Blockchain Olympiad where they are pitted against the very best from around the world.

No registration or participation costs.

Why Compete?

As we move into an ever growing online world, students will find themselves in a job market where blockchain will be generating over HK$24 trillion by 2030 across all industry verticals. The Blockchain Olympiad offers not only a strong foundation for these pathways but also valuable experience in critical thinking and problem solving.
